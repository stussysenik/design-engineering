# VISION

As of April 1, 2026.

## North Star

Become the engineer people call when a product is:

- technically complex
- operationally messy
- visually underpowered
- interaction-heavy
- AI-inflected
- important enough to deserve taste

That is the lane.

Not “I know many languages.”
Not “I can do a little of everything.”

The lane is:

`high-leverage product systems with exceptional experience quality`

## The One Thing To Double Down On

If you want one super skill, make it this:

`interaction systems for complex tools`

That includes:

- information architecture
- state transitions
- trust and visibility
- motion with purpose
- keyboard and gesture fluency
- latency handling
- composable primitives
- operational clarity

This skill is rare, portable, and senior.

It also bridges directly across:

- iOS
- web apps
- internal tools
- CLI/TUI
- AI products
- workflow software

## Role Trajectory

### Closest High-Probability Path

- Design Engineer
- Experience Design Engineer
- Product Engineer on AI/devtools/workflow teams
- Senior iOS / macOS product engineer
- Staff product engineer with strong UX ownership

### More Advanced Paths Later

#### If You Want Graphics / Rendering Depth

You will need public proof in:

- real-time rendering
- 3D math
- profiling and frame budgets
- Metal or native graphics APIs
- scene graphs, animation systems, or shader pipelines

#### If You Want CV / Applied AI Depth

You will need public proof in:

- on-device inference
- data quality and evals
- model behavior analysis
- failure modes
- benchmarking
- robust productization, not just demos

#### If You Want Principal / MTS-Level Product Systems

You will need public proof in:

- sustained ownership of a system others adopt
- architecture and tradeoff writing
- APIs and primitives other engineers build on
- reliability and iteration over time
- clear influence on product direction, not just implementation

## Should You Learn Things From Atomic Units?

Yes, but selectively.

You do not need to build every layer from scratch.
You do need to understand enough of the stack to reason from first principles.

The right pattern:

- pick one vertical problem
- understand it down to the metal where necessary
- ship the product-level abstraction

That is better than broad “from scratch” tourism.

This is the best bridge between your public profile and companies like Windmill.

Should include:

- CLI + web handoff
- run history and artifacts
- plans, diffs, logs, previews

Could target:

`AI-native iOS utility`

Not chat.
Not toy image gen.

Something that turns camera, screenshot, or captured context into structured action:

- classify
- extract
- review

- track
- decide

This is the cleanest bridge into Apple-native product roles.

## What To Write

Write less, but better.

The writing program:

- 1 essay per flagship project
- 1 systems note per quarter
- 1 teardown of an interface decision you changed your mind about

Good recurring format:

- the problem
- the failure mode
- the design principle
- the implementation
- the tradeoff
- the before/after artifact

That gives you credibility.

## What Not To Over-Invest In

- gradients for their own sake
- endless clone work
- language churn as identity
- mini-experiments published immediately
- prestige topics without a product angle
